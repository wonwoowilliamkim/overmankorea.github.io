My research seeks to understand how institutions translate individual intentions into socially beneficial outcomes. I am especially interested in how institutional design and incentive structures channel behavior and welfare across health, labor, and political settings.

By applying microeconomic theory and empirical methods, my goal is to develop frameworks that clarify the mechanisms through which institutional arrangements operate and to apply these insights to evidence-based policy design.

I am currently a Research Assistant working with Prof. Hans van Kippersluis and Prof. Robert Dur at Erasmus School of Economics.

Working Papers & Work in Progress

  • Dynamic Mechanism for Return Incentives in Asylum Systems
    Work in Progress with Prof. Robert Dur Erasmus University Rotterdam · Aug 2025 – Present
    Developing a sequential screening model of return payments for asylum seekers. The project extends time-inconsistency frameworks to analyze how credibility constraints shape migration decisions and optimal policy design.
  • Optimal Taxation of E-Cigarette Products
    Work in Progress with Prof. Hans van Kippersluis Erasmus University Rotterdam · Jun 2025 – Present
    Building a welfare-based model to determine the optimal e-cigarette excise tax for the Netherlands. The analysis incorporates cross-border arbitrage and estimates Dutch-specific demand elasticities through spatial variation.
  • Poll Weighting Effects in Intra-Party Primaries on Electoral Strategy and Outcomes
    Completed with Prof. Rhee Keeyoung Sungkyunkwan University · Feb 2025
    Developed a game-theoretic model of intra-party primaries analyzing candidate positioning across configurations. Simulated equilibria characterize how poll weighting and entry constraints moderate political extremism.
  • Gender-Specific Cardiovascular Screening Intervention Design
    Completed with Prof. Hans van Kippersluis Erasmus University Rotterdam · Feb 2025
    Constructed a disease–age–gender panel from CBS data for Zuid-Holland municipalities. Designed a targeted intervention to address gender-specific screening attitudes. Findings presented at a symposium to clinicians, data scientists, and insurance representatives.
  • Effects of Childbirth Incentives by Local Governments on Birth Rate
    Completed with Prof. Lee Dongwon Sungkyunkwan University · Aug 2024
    Constructed and analyzed a South Korean municipal panel dataset on local government childbirth incentives. Estimated the causal impact of financial incentives for first- and second-born children on fertility rates using panel methods.
